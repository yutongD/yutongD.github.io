BLOSSOM

A women's period tracking app.

  • Teamwork
  • April 2019 - June 2019

    Pen & paper, Online survey builder (Survey Star), Axure RP

Blossom evolved out of the Design of User Interface course. We were required to do user research and completed the final prototype in collaboration with other students. Blossom is a women's health app that helps women track their period & ovulation.

The idea was inspired by my female friends’ complaints about irregular periods and my personal experiences of unhelpful tracking apps. We hope this app can accompany each life stage of women.

What we learned from the survey:

Male Users

Under the assumption that men will have or already have female lovers, more than sixty percent of them think that considering male demands is needed. Interestingly, fewer single men felt this way. Overall, as men get older they start to have their own families and tend to care more about women’s issues.

The period-related issues women are most concerned about

The issues women are most concerned about—as set out in the graph—helped us understand users' needs and drove the design of the period-recording page on the app.



Paid subscription services

The percentage of young women who are unwilling to subscribe to paid content is larger than for mothers.
Unexpectedly, all users (103 in total) who didn’t think the app should consider men's needs were not willing to pay for content.

THEY ARE TOO PROACTIVE

Many period-tracking apps require constant thinking about time tracking as a boring task without any feedback. You open the app and click to start the cycle, and then open the app again and click to end it. These apps are competing with things that are more important in people’s lives, rather than engaging people. So they lose users.


DISCUSSING MY PERIOD WITH OTHERS
MAKES ME UNCOMFORTABLE



Typically, people feel awkward talking to others about personal matters, especially embarrassing symptoms or personal behaviors. In contrast, people tend to speak freely online to strangers. People tend to be more candid when nobody knows who they are, and they can say anything without fear.


IT IS NO DIFFERENT TO ADDING EVENTS
ON AN ORDINARY CALENDAR

Currently, people are quick to uninstall an app and try a new one. We can’t expect much patience from users. What they need is period analysis and prediction, as well as personal advice generated from the recording of their period. Asking users to report daily matters on period is meaningless. They want a period assistant rather than a tracker.


I KNOW THEY ARE HELPFUL,
BUT [THEY ARE] ... TOO WORDY, TOO BORING.



The defining characteristic of the modern reader is impatience. Healthcare videos and articles are helpful,but no one will spend a lot of time watching or reading them.






Step 3: Persona



DESIGN

Goals

The next step was to transform the discoveries I had made into specific goals. I asked myself:


How can we strengthen the connection with users?
What if the recording a period is as easy as clicking on a button?
What if the app specifies tailored and targeted health plans for them?
What if the app provides a safe environment for users to express themselves anonymously?


I was determined to create a better solution.


Style of the app

Warm, cheerful interface and graphic design.
Simple and well-organized functional areas.
Easy-to-find period-tracking module.


The app’s features

An semi-anonymous community for open discussion and asking questions.
Short videos about women's health that can be watched in short fragmented time.
Period recording must be very simple and requires little user input.
Accurate period analysis.


User flow

With the requirements I had distilled from the research, I designed the mind map. I drew a sitemap to map out the basic layout of the functions I wanted to include in the app.

THE FINAL PROTOTYPE



JUST A CLICK
Period tracking consists of a series of buttons for inputting information without typing.




PRIVACY
When users ask or answer questions anonymously, the system randomly assigns a nickname to them, such as "banana."


HEALTHCARE KNOWLEDGE
We provide two forms of healthcare videos. Short videos of 4–5 minutes that can be watched in short fragmented time, and live videos enable real-time interaction with quick feedback.
